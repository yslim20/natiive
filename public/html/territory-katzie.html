Katzie [KUT-zee]

Cities on this Territory:

  • Pitt Watershed
  • Alouette watershed
  • Pitt Meadows
  • Maple Ridge
  • Surrey
  • Langley

Languages Spoken:

  • Hən̓q̓əmin̓əm̓

The Katzie people have been living here since long ago, living around the areas now known as Pitt Lake, Sheridan Hill, port Hammond, Port Roberts, and Point Grey. They have fished sockeye and other salmon species from a variety of fishing stations and seasonal villages along the rivers for years.

The Katzie community is growing, however, and is continuing its efforts to preserve a place for itself in its traditional territory. Currently, there is approximately 592 members of the Katzie first nation, with about 302 of the people living on their five reserves. Katzie maintains a strong local presence in the salmon fisheries, and Katzie people continue to hunt throughout the territory and utilize the landscape as best they can.
